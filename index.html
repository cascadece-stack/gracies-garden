Mushroom of the Month

Lion’s Mane

Lion’s Mane (Hericium erinaceus) is a distinctive white, shaggy mushroom known for its unique appearance and delicious flavor. Often found growing on hardwood trees, it’s a prized find for foragers. This mushroom is not only valued for its mild, seafood-like taste but also for its potential cognitive benefits. Fun fact: Lion’s Mane has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries and is a favorite among wild mushroom hunters.

Try this simple Lion’s Mane recipe: Lion’s Mane Sauté. Tear the mushroom into bite-sized pieces, heat a pan with a bit of butter or oil, and sauté until golden brown. Add garlic, a splash of soy sauce, and fresh herbs to taste. Enjoy as a savory side or a topping for toast!
